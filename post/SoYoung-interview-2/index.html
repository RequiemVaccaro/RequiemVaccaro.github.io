【采访】Dead Pretties

2025-10-08

Josh Whettingsteel Speaks to Dead Pretties

原载于：So Young
作者：Josh Whettingsteel

Oscar, Jacob and Ben are Dead Pretties and ever since they played our festival last year we’ve been eagerly awaiting their first release. That release came early this year in the form of ‘Social Experiment’. The song shouting aloud what everyone in the room is thinking about everyone else. We caught up with the boys from Seven Sisters to learn more about the band keeping ‘The Scene’ on its toes…

How did the band form?

Oscar and Jacob had a shared hatred of music college and after dropping out decided to recruit Ben and we started making music on our own terms. Jacob had previously met Ben at Reading Festival, Ben recalls that at the time Jacob was without a pair of shoes or a pot to piss in. Jacob says it wasn’t his fault that he forgot to bring a tent.

‘Social Experiment’ has a really refreshing feel and got great response online. Is that sound a sign of things to come?

It’s one thing we do but we don’t want to limit ourselves to one thing, we write in different styles too and lyrically it always differs, social commentary seems to be a running theme, although not intentionally, its just happened that way. We’re not a strictly political band or anything like that.

What inspired the lyrics for ‘Social Experiment’?

I was disillusioned with the general fakery present in social circles/situations that I’d found myself in, people compromising their true self to suit whoever they’re talking to, switching colours like cowardly chameleons. This seems to be a common trait with our generation – it’s dull and it’s pointless.

How do you feel about the South London scene you've come out of? Is there safety in the group?

It’s great that there’s loads of young bands putting on exciting shows but I seriously doubt that anyone's looking for safety, we all happen to be from the same city which has no doubt helped the ‘hype’ (whatever that means) all the bands would stand up by themselves regardless of geographic location. All the music is good, it nods to the group. If the safety you refer to is the kind implied in our release then I don’t think that is present in London’s music scene at all. That kind of safety can make you feel too comfortable and then the whole scene would stagnate and begin to lack the quality of creative output we have seen thus far. I don’t think that is going to happen.

Are there any groups you’re fond of that you use as a blueprint for Dead Pretties? Any meaningful influences you’d like to reach?

Maybe when we started out we tried (probably a bit too hard) to be like bands we were listening to at the time, but I think that limited us in the sense that we thought we had to write in a certain style. Our best songs have come out when we haven’t given much thought to what they sound like or what they should sound like. No blueprints. What we want to do is continue to build a fan base of people who are purely interested in the songs, what we’re playing and saying/singing, nothing else. If you have that behind you, you’ve won as far as we’re concerned, fuck mainstream or any other means to gauge ones success. If something comes along to boost our popularity we’re not going to cover our nose up at that but that’s not the reason why we play music.

What can we expect as far as releases and festivals this year?

We’re going to be playing as many shows as possible, especially in summer so I'm sure you'll see me in a lot of festivals, as far as releases are concerned: WATCH THIS SPACE.
